# Plans & Proposals — 2026

## 1. Strategic Initiatives

### 1.1 Service Line Development

Continue all current initiatives and expand:

- **Low-code (Bubble.io)** — Build on SamTrack success (GPM 60%), close Stembridge, expand client workshops
- **ERPNext** — Evaluate as a new service line for existing client needs
- **QA Mobile Testing** — Scale the service line launched in 2025
- **Typo3 Development** — Continue growing the PHP service offering
- **Odoo** — Complete internal migration, position as client-facing service
- **New service lines** — AI-assisted tooling makes it faster and cheaper to evaluate and launch new offerings; continue identifying opportunities

### 1.2 Joint Marketing

- Continue and expand the Adexin partnership (joint pre-sales, shared leads)
- Pursue the German client opportunity currently in progress

### 1.3 Unlocking Smaller Projects

Align with Sales to stop rejecting smaller/cheaper projects. With low-code and active AI assistance, previously unprofitable projects can become viable:

- Establish a strict, lean process to minimize effort on smaller engagements
- Process them quickly with AI-assisted estimation, development, and delivery
- Keep them profitable by controlling scope and using efficient tooling
- Expands the addressable pipeline without adding headcount

### 1.4 AI at Company Scale

Scale AI initiatives beyond development and QA:

- **AI for Company Operations** — Apply AI tooling to internal processes, efficiency gains across departments
- **AI for RFPs** — Use AI assistance to improve speed and quality of proposal responses, increasing win rate on incoming requests
- The company needs more projects — AI can help compete more effectively for them

## 2. Team & Financial Goals

Market context: incoming client requests have slowed and some teams have reduced. In this environment:

- **Maintain team size** — Target keeping the current ~31 contractors active
- **Grow GPM through optimization** — Provide more value with the same resources by improving utilization, reducing idle time, and expanding scope within existing client engagements
- **Focus on retention** — Protect the team built over the past years; replacing people in a slow market is harder than keeping them

## 3. Flexibility

Open to proposals from the CTO on structure, responsibilities, or new areas of ownership. The goal is to remain flexible and adapt to what the company needs most.

## 4. Compensation Proposal

**Requested adjustment:** Hourly rate increase from **35 EUR/hr to 40 EUR/hr** (+14.3%)

| Metric | Current | Proposed |
|---|---|---|
| Hourly rate | 35 EUR/hr | 40 EUR/hr |
| Base (monthly) | 5,680 EUR/mo | ~6,480 EUR/mo |
| Bonus | 760 EUR/mo (GPM-dependent) | 760 EUR/mo (unchanged — tied to GPM, not base) |
| Total (monthly) | 6,440 EUR/mo | ~7,240 EUR/mo |

**Note:** Bonus is calculated based on department GPM performance, not as a percentage of the base rate. It remains unchanged unless GPM grows.

**Context:** This adjustment moves the base from below the standard EM market floor into the lower portion of the standard EM range (6,400-8,700 EUR/mo), while the actual role scope remains aligned with the Head of Engineering tier (8,300-11,300 EUR/mo). The request is conservative relative to the market data.
